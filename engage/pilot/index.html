Why duration matters

Canopy pockets are cycle-driven

If you don’t catch the trigger window, the average looks “fine.” Cycles are where pockets show up, repeat, and prove they’re real.

  • Triggers reveal pockets — averages hide them.
  • Short pilots = find + locate.
  • Long pilots = repeatability + recurrence.
  • Best signal is late-flower + night cycles.

Why this matters

Why These Pilots Are Worth Doing

These pilots exist because the failure modes are common, fast, and expensive—and “average RH” rarely catches them in time.

  • Canopy pockets are common.
  • Powdery mildew pressure is an industry staple.
  • Botrytis (“bud rot”) can move fast under high RH.
  • Compliance failures (yeast/mold) and recalls are real.
  • Rules vary by state (including Aspergillus targets in some regimes).
