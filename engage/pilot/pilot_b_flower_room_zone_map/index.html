Why this matters in flower

Flower pockets diverge from averages

PM and botrytis risk is tightly coupled to humidity dynamics and airflow—exactly what diverges inside dense canopies.

High-RH windows can happen inside canopy

Literature reports rapid botrytis damage under high RH at moderate temperatures—microclimates can hit those windows even when averages look “safe.”

Flower runs on triggers

Lights, irrigation, night control, dehu cycles, HVAC limits—Pilot B measures across those triggers and verifies beyond one lucky day.

How to read the deliverables

Pocket catalog (ranked)

Use this to answer: Which pockets matter most operationally? Ranking is built from persistence, severity, and timing vs your cycle.

Operator pattern: start with the top 1–2 pockets by persistence + severity, align timing to triggers, then choose production-realistic interventions.

Repeatability scoring

Use this to answer: Do hotspots recur under comparable triggers? Repeatability separates structural problems from noise.

Decision rule: we only call a pocket “repeatable” when location + rank are broadly stable across comparable cycles and coverage.

Intervention tests (before/after + pass/fail)

Use this to answer: Did the change measurably improve the pocket zones? Reported with pass/fail criteria in safe-to-interpret windows.

Practical use: if a test fails, we don’t reinterpret it—we change the intervention and re-verify against the same criteria.

Zone strategy (what to do next)

Use this to answer: Where should we adjust airflow and dehu strategy? Actions are tied to zones that repeat and respond to tests.

Operator meaning: this is the handoff artifact—clear enough to execute, specific enough to avoid “turn everything down 2% and pray.”

Receipts + validity (trust-first evidence)

Use this to answer: Can I trust this comparison? Receipts record what ran; validity gating marks safe vs abstain windows.

Practical takeaway: if something changed (layout/coverage/calibration/cycle mismatch), the system flags it—so you don’t over-claim wins.

FAQ

Is this “more sensors”?

No. The point is better interpretation: alignment/validity checks and verification evidence that explain when to trust the reading and whether a change actually worked.

What changes do you test?

Typical tests include airflow adjustments, dehu strategy changes, and placement corrections—chosen jointly with the operator and constrained by what’s realistic in production.
