Pilot D — PM / Botrytis Defense

Recurrence tracking + A/B tests + QA-grade evidence.

This is a prevention pilot. It’s priced and structured differently because the burden is different: you’re not buying a map—you’re buying proof that the fix holds across triggers and time.

Why this exists (with references)

  • PM is widely documented as favored by humidity dynamics and poor circulation/overcrowding—recurring enough to be a staple problem.
  • Peer-reviewed cannabis work reports Botrytis can rapidly destroy inflorescences under high RH (e.g., >70%) and moderate temperatures.
  • Compliance failures tied to mold/yeast limits and large recalls have been reported; broader investigations describe ongoing mold risk in legal markets.

Deliverables (QA-grade)

  • Recurrence report: what repeats, where, and under which triggers
  • Risk-zone dashboard: pockets + persistence + change history
  • A/B efficacy package: before/after evidence with pass/fail criteria
  • Validity + abstains: explicit “safe to interpret/cancel” windows (and reasons when not)
  • Audit-ready receipts: versions, calibration state, configuration, and validity uptime

Typical timeline (60–90 days)

  1. Baseline: commissioning + initial zone map + pocket catalog
  2. Recurrence: repeated capture across cycles (especially night control and late flower)
  3. A/B cycles: multiple intervention tests (airflow, dehu strategy, placement fixes)
  4. Evidence: QA-grade writeups + pass/fail boundaries + “no-go” conditions

Acceptance tests (examples)

  • Recurrence confirmed: pockets recur predictably under defined triggers.
  • Fix efficacy: intervention reduces pocket severity and/or persistence with measurable margin.
  • Fix holds: efficacy persists across comparable cycles and time windows.
  • Abstain logic: system explicitly flags when measurement conditions make conclusions unsafe.

Best fit

  • You have recurring PM pressure or late-flower botrytis risk.
  • A single remediation/downgrade event is financially brutal.
  • You need defensible proof (QA, leadership, partners, or post-mortems).
