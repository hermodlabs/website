Why this exists (with references)

Humidity dynamics + poor circulation

PM is widely documented as favored by humidity dynamics and poor circulation/overcrowding—recurring enough to be a staple problem.

Botrytis can move fast under high RH

Compliance narratives demand receipts

Recalls and compliance failures tied to mold/yeast limits have been reported; prevention needs defensible evidence, not arguments.

How to read the deliverables

Recurrence report

Use this to answer: Is it truly recurring—and what triggers it? Repeatable hotspot geometry under comparable triggers is the basis for prevention-grade claims.

Practical use: if recurrence only appears under a specific window, that window becomes the intervention test target.

Risk-zone dashboard

Use this to answer: What is the current risk state—and how has it changed? Operator + QA view: pockets, persistence, timing cues, and change history.

Decision pattern: focus on pockets that are both persistent and severe; treat one-off spikes differently than always-wet zones.

A/B efficacy package (pass/fail)

Use this to answer: Did the intervention reduce severity/persistence—with margin? Structured for go/no-go decisions.

QA framing: a win is measurable reduction in safe-to-interpret windows—and it survives re-checks.

Validity + abstains (safe-to-interpret)

Use this to answer: When are conclusions safe—and when should we abstain? Abstain keeps evidence defensible under drift.

Operator meaning: we don’t claim efficacy when conditions aren’t trustworthy—even if plots look nicer.

Audit-ready receipts (provenance)

Use this to answer: What exactly ran—and how much was valid? Receipts support partner review, QA sign-off, and post-mortems.

Practical use: if someone disputes results (“not the same setup”), receipts prove what changed and what did not.
