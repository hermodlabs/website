Pilot E — Founder Cohort

Year-long drift control, receipts, and institutional memory.

Pilot E is subscription-style because the value is not a baseline map. It’s sustained control: recurring verification windows, change tracking, and a maintained operating standard as rooms, staff, and seasons change.

Platform status / scope note: This pilot page reflects the current Pilot E operating approach and report format. Outputs, UI screens, terminology, and acceptance tests may evolve; any engagement is governed by the statement of work and acceptance criteria agreed for your site.

Why this is rational (operator version)

  • Microclimate-driven disease and compliance risk is persistent; the goal is not one win, but preventing drift and recurring pockets over time.
  • When you scale, the cost is regressions + re-learning—seasonal drift, staff changes, equipment swaps, and room-to-room variation.
  • Pilot E buys continuity: repeatable verification, early drift detection with context, and a maintained operating standard.
  • The difference vs a mapping pilot: Pilot E is built for sustained control—recurring verification windows, change tracking, and receipts that survive turnover.

Best fit

  • You’re expanding (more rooms, more sites, more shifts).
  • You keep regressing after “fixes” because reality changes (seasonal drift, staff changes, equipment swaps).
  • You want an operating standard that survives turnover and scale.

What you get (cadence-based)

  • Monthly: recurring verification window(s)
  • Continuous: drift + change tracking (what moved, where, when)
  • Continuous: validity uptime + receipts
  • Quarterly: exec readout (what improved, what regressed, what’s next)
  • As needed: SOP and training artifacts updates
  • Ongoing: roadmap influence (what to build next based on your facility reality)

How to read the deliverables

Recurring verification windows (monthly cadence)

Use this to answer: Are we re-checking under comparable conditions, on purpose? Verification windows turn “we think it’s fixed” into repeatable proof across months.

Practical meaning: if conditions drift, the cadence catches it—before the room teaches you the lesson again.

Drift + change tracking (what moved, where, when)

Use this to answer: What changed—and did that change move the pockets? This makes regressions early and contextual (not surprise late-flower events).

Decision pattern: treat “new drift after an equipment swap” differently than “slow seasonal drift.”

Validity uptime + receipts (continuous audit trail)

Use this to answer: How much of the run was valid—and what exactly was true during those windows? Receipts make the history defensible: versions, calibration state, configuration, and validity uptime.

Operator meaning: we don’t “claim success” outside of safe-to-interpret windows—so evidence survives scrutiny.

Quarterly exec readout (improved vs regressed vs next)

Use this to answer: What got better, what got worse, and what do we do next? The readout is structured to drive decisions: protect improvements, address regressions, and prioritize next actions.

Practical use: leadership sees progress and regressions in one place—without turning it into an argument about anecdotes.

SOP + training artifacts updates (institutional memory)

Use this to answer: Can new people and new rooms reproduce the method? This is the institutional memory layer: updated SOPs, templates, and training artifacts that keep the standard stable.

Institutionalization: the method becomes reproducible—not dependent on one person’s memory.

Roadmap influence (build-next based on facility reality)

Use this to answer: What should we build next—based on real facility signals? Pilot E provides a feedback loop from on-site reality into roadmap priorities.

The point: fewer hypothetical features—more targeted fixes driven by measured facility reality.

Acceptance tests (examples)

Use this to answer: What does “it holds” mean here? Pilot E defines the minimum bar for institutionalization, early drift control, and an audit trail that persists over time.

  • Institutionalization: new rooms and new staff can reproduce the method.
  • Drift control: regressions are detected early with context (not surprise late-flower events).
  • Audit trail: receipts and change history exist over time (versions, configs, validity).
