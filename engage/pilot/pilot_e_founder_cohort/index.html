How to read the deliverables

Recurring verification windows (monthly cadence)

Use this to answer: Are we re-checking under comparable conditions, on purpose? Verification windows turn “we think it’s fixed” into repeatable proof across months.

Practical meaning: the cadence catches drift before the room teaches you the lesson again.

Drift + change tracking (what moved, where, when)

Use this to answer: What changed—and did that change move the pockets? Make regressions early and contextual (not late-flower surprises).

Decision pattern: treat “new drift after a swap” differently than “slow seasonal drift.”

Validity uptime + receipts (continuous audit trail)

Use this to answer: How much of the run was valid—and what exactly was true during those windows? Receipts make history defensible.

Operator meaning: we don’t claim success outside safe-to-interpret windows—so evidence survives scrutiny.

Quarterly exec readout (improved vs regressed vs next)

Use this to answer: What got better, what got worse, and what’s next? Drive decisions across rooms/sites.

Practical use: leadership sees progress and regressions in one place—without anecdote fights.

SOP + training artifacts updates (institutional memory)

Use this to answer: Can new people and new rooms reproduce the method? Updates keep “good” stable through turnover.

Institutionalization: the method becomes reproducible—not dependent on one person’s memory.

Roadmap influence (build-next based on facility reality)

Use this to answer: What should we build next—based on real facility signals? Feedback loop from on-site reality into priorities.

The point: fewer hypothetical features—more targeted fixes driven by measured facility reality.
