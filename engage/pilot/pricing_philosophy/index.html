Pricing

Pricing Philosophy

We price pilots by scope, cycles observed, and verification burden. A one-time map is cheap. Proving a fix holds across triggers is the work.

The five drivers behind pricing

We price pilots like ops work. The price scales with:

  • Scope
  • Time-on-site
  • Verification
  • Stakeholders
  • Risk / QA burden

Each step up buys more touches, more cycles, and more accountability artifacts.

  • 1 Scope of measurement geometry

    One room vs multiple rooms. More rooms multiplies capture, analysis, and reporting.

  • 2 Verification is the expensive part

    Before/after runs, pass/fail checks, “what moved,” and A/B tests — this is where labor stacks.

  • 3 Time horizon cycles observed

    Pockets are cycle-driven. You need enough triggers + recurrence to trust the conclusion.

  • 4 Deliverable sophistication

    Ops vs QA vs executive artifacts change how defensible the output is.

  • 5 Capacity, access, and support logins

    More stakeholders means training, handoffs, and support load.

Plain English: a one-time map is cheap. Proving the fix holds across triggers is the work.

When each tier is rational
