Why this order is operator-correct

Operators don’t start by asking for recurrence scores. They start by asking for relief. Relief begins with location and timing. “Where is it failing?” and “When does it happen?”

Once you can point at a zone and a trigger window, you can do real work: change airflow paths, adjust dehu strategy, tweak staging, fix SOP timing. Then recurrence and verification matter — because you’re trying to prove the room changed, not just the dashboard.

This is also why duration matters: short pilots find, long pilots prove. If you can’t show repeatability, people call it luck. If you can’t show before/after, people call it vibes.
