About HermodLabs

When sensor data gets weird, the problem is rarely the data.

It’s the interpretation.

We built HermodLabs after spending enough time inside Corporate America to recognize the pattern: when streams don’t agree, the first response is almost never “our assumptions broke.” It’s “the sensor is flaky,” “the room changed,” “the model needs tuning,” or “ops did something different.”

The dashboard stays calm while the explanation gets rebuilt. The pipeline starts supplying its own justification. A plot becomes evidence for the assumptions required to interpret the plot.

That’s when the consultants arrive. Not to fix the measurement problem. To fix the story problem.

Consulting Rule #1

If the data won’t authorize the story, make the story authorize the data.

Consulting Rule #2

If the results won’t validate your promises, reinterpret the results.

Hypothetical use case that certainly couldn’t happen in your company #1: Baine & Cope shows up with a fresh deck and a 78-page “Alignment Memo.” Page 3 has your exact phrasing in quotes. Page 41 has a new definition of “stable.” The chart is unchanged. The conclusion is bold.

Hypothetical use case that certainly couldn’t happen in your company #2: McQuincey & Associates runs the post-mortem with the KPI slide already formatted. The red line gets a new baseline. The pass threshold gets a new footnote. The scope quietly shrinks to “Phase 1.” The metrics are green again.

HermodLabs exists to break that loop. We make interpretation earned. We turn the fine print into an explicit contract stack—so claims attach to checks that can fail, with receipts that survive a different operator, a different day, and a different bench.

  • No self-authorizing plots. Interpretation is allowed only when the contract stack passes.
  • Receipts, not vibes. Runs produce artifacts you can audit later, not just screenshots.
  • Failure is diagnosable. When it breaks, the pipeline tells you what broke—first.
