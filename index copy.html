Hidden micro-zones

Field pattern (common)

Humidors develop local pockets: corners near doors, upper shelves, dead-air regions behind displays, and spots near vents or fans. The “average RH” can stay stable while those pockets drift.

That’s how inventory gets quietly stressed: wrappers dry, burn changes, and you only notice after enough product has cycled through the bad zone.

We model humidity as a field, not a single number — so you can see which zones are drifting, when they drift, and how fast.

Door cycles & airflow paths

Mechanism

Every door opening creates a transient flow: drier air enters, humid air exits, and the room re-equilibrates unevenly depending on geometry and fan placement.

The result is not “RH goes down a bit.” It’s a drift path that can repeatedly hit the same shelves or corners.

Our system tracks changes over time so you can correlate drift with events and layout.

Inventory quality & consistency

Business impact

Consistency matters. If the same product lives in different zones, customers can experience different draws and burns from the “same” cigar.

A spatial map helps you manage placement, rotation, and conditioning routines based on what the room is actually doing.

This is instrumentation for the room — not just a number on the wall.

How it works

We use multiple humidity observations around the room to reconstruct a 3D humidity field. The output is a map and time series: where the room drifts, how it drifts, and which zones repeatedly go out of range.
