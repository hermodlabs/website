Exclusion Contract

Lock in your vertical. If you secure exclusivity first, competitors may lose access.

What It Is

An exclusion contract reserves HermodLabs' work in a specific industry vertical for your organization. You get priority access, defined scope, and a clear path from “messy signals” to measurable, auditable outputs.

Why It Exists

  • Timing becomes leverage. Once verification and cancellation are repeatable, the same engine can be deployed across many sites.
  • Bench-to-infrastructure. You don't want a one-off demo. You want a capability your org can reuse and audit.
  • First mover advantage. If your competitor reserves the vertical first, you may lose access.

How It Works

  1. Define the vertical. We write down what counts as “in scope” (and what doesn't).
  2. Run a qualification call. We confirm your problem is actually timing/drift-shaped.
  3. Set milestones. Bench definition, commissioning, acceptance tests, and deliverables.
  4. Reserve the lane. Once executed, we won't take equivalent work in that vertical for the contract term.

What You Get

  • Priority access to the co-timing engine and our roadmap for your vertical.
  • Pilot / deployment plan with a defined install footprint and commissioning workflow.
  • Receipts: diagnostics + a simple certificate of what was assumed, what was tested, and why to trust the output.
  • A repeatable playbook you can re-run across sites (not a one-off “lucky notch”).

Good Fit If

  • You have multiple sensors/streams that should match but don't.
  • Your current approach works “sometimes,” and failures are hard to diagnose.
  • You need outputs that survive audits, not just screenshots.
  • Drift quietly becomes cost: scrap, downtime, SLA risk, or safety risk.
